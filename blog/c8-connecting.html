Connecting

11 may 18

Have you tried using any of the insight and techniques for growing trust and sincerity? How did it go?

When I was training people at McDonald's, I would always make an effort to connect with them on an empathetic-level. I found that after being courteous to them, or standing up for them -- as well as facilitating their learning -- they begun to trust me more than the manager's (lmao). Often I'd get new crew come to me, as they were to intimidated or scared to ask management -- which I saw as a product of compassion.

Try empathic listening - how did it go?

To draw on from the last question... you'd often be required to listen to what new crew were experiencing; being able to reflect and speak back to them, in a way which mirrored what they were feeling was a good way to connect and build trust.

I think in general, without the construct of an "exercise", people are often too scared to say what they are feeling, in fear they may get shot-down, or "deflected". I feel personally, that empathic listening can go a long way in helping reassure someone, or help them (even marginally).

How did you find the loving kindness meditation?

I felt it was a great way to feel and build empathy, especially towards someone you care about. It's a way of understanding that regardless of what you may be feeling now -- or have felt in the past -- that this person has likely had similar experiences.

Complete the political awareness exercise here in writing.

Think of a difficult situation from your present or past, when there was some conflict or disagreement, something real, something that has some meaning and potency for you.

Keeping it related to my time at McDonald's lol, there was a situation where I offered to remedy a customer's issue with free stuff! To me, the issue that the customer had was relatively minor -- i.e., it was being blown out of proportion, but I realised that there's no point in trying to argue, as it would only slow service down (and probably anger the customer more).
When the manager on floor realised I had given away something for free (trying to do something courteous / nice), they were immediately angry and threatened me.

To me, I was only trying to be courteous, and if it appeases both parties involved then it is a good result. After explaining my decision-making to the manager afterwards, she apologised and agreed that she could see where I was coming from.

Conversely, I could see where she was coming from; being more concerned about the long-term benefits of giving stuff away.
