When programming, what are some of your triggers? What causes a disproportionate emotional response?

Definitely after hitting a road-block when solving a problem. Frustration definitely kicks in if it's a simple error, but I've learnt to take silly mistakes -- and any mistakes in general -- into learning-experiences, which has helped me a lot presently, and hopefully in the long run.

Here's a pertinent quote from Search Inside Yourself:

“If you are distressed by anything external, the pain is not due to the thing itself, but to your estimate of it; and this you have the power to revoke at any moment.”

Taking a break tends to alleviate any immediate distress that hitting a roadblock causes; tying in with this quote.

How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?

Whenever you feel triggered, just stop.

Bringing to mind an unpleasant event from the past definitely brought on lots of negative emotions. Stopping -- and breathing -- immediately alleviated most of those unhappy emotions for me, and reflecting definitely helped. As in the first question, taking a break for me helps immensely whenever I'm experiencing emotional distress, and is in general a great first option when after being triggered emotionally. It let my mind settle without provoking, or aggravating the thought in my head; and reflection tied in well with previous mindfulness practice.

Conversely, I did find it hard to stay completely settled for the entire duration, but found the whole experience pretty helpful, even if I (and most / some people) might do something similar sub-consciously.
