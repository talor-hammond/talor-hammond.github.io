Design to Web

28 mar 18

What is a responsive site? Why is responsiveness important?

In short, a responsive site -- or responsive web design -- ensures that the website looks good on all devices, i.e. devices with different screen resolutions / displays.

It's important as devices all have different screen-sizes, and w responsive web design our sites can cater towards all of these. Static web design would limit the reach your site would have.

What is mobile first design? Why is it important?

Mobile first design is one of the strategies used when building a responsive website. It is the idea of building a design for a mobile-display first, as it is considered the most difficult (compared to building for desktop / bigger displays). "Once the mobile design questions are answered, designing for other devices will be easier."

Mobile first design can significantly cut down the load-time for more graphic / design-intensive websites.

What are frameworks? Pros and cons?

Frameworks make our life easier as front-end developers, with preset CSS / styles allowing us to build our grid-based designs without having to write our own code / structures.

Frameworks are great for efficiency, and for building prototypes in small amounts of time; allowing you to build clean and symmetrical layouts. For larger-scale websites, there will generally be limitations around the framework's core behaviours. i.e., it restricts your freedom as a developer, and forces you to use the framework's semantics.

What is a wireframe, and why do we use it?

A wireframe -- in terms of web design -- is a basic visual representation of elements on a website.

We use it, as it serves as a blueprint for how our design should look, with the main focus not on colour or graphics, but functionality, behaviour, and content priority.

Any difficults with implementing your wireframe?
