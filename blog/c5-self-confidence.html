What is the relationship between mindfulness and self-awareness?

In essence, self-awareness and mindfulness are the same thing -- take this analogy from chapter-four of Search Inside Yourself: "The traditional analogy of this mind is a fluttering flag. The flag represents the mind. In the presence of strong emotions, the mind may be turbulent like a flag fluttering violently in the wind.”
The flagpole represents mindfulness -- it keeps the mind steady despite all that emotional movement. In that, mindfulness is similar to self-awareness: it allows us to view ourselves with "third-person objectivity".

Try at least one of the self-awareness practices from the chapter (Body Scan, Journaling).
What was your experience of it?
How did you feel?

After trying the "body scan" technique covered in the book initially, I felt I wasn't able to settle properly / focus on specific areas well enough -- which I think was due to being around displays / monitors, and noise. I tried it again before sleeping, after I had put down my phone / laptop for the night: it was a great process for settling my mind / head before sleep, and finding myself being acutely aware of feelings involved with different areas of the body. Will definitely trial again before sleeping to see if it helps.

What did you take away from the "My Emotions are Not Me" section?

The quote -- "If my emotions are who I am, then there is very little I can do about it" -- stood out to me: with mindfulness and self-awareness, our emotions are not simply a part of us, but what we feel in our bodies; from existential to experiental.
