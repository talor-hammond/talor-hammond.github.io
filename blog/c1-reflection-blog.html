Cultural

Reflection

10 mar 18

What's your take on the DBC / EDA experience?

In short -- it looks, and feels like an awesome learning-environment. Shereef's words: "ownership over the environment" really stood out to me. Ultimately, the result of this experience will be up to myself; what is learnt, created, developed -- a true transformational rite of passage.

What are your impressions?

Learning about the dynamic-learning mindset individuals must have to get the most out of anything is really reassuring. Highly-concentrated learning groups are extremely important in narrowing the knowledge gap between individuals; something that is essential when learning so much within a short amount of time. Learning of time-boxing, and organisational-theory was really eye-opening -- definitely something I will take advantage of. Further, understanding that intangibles like soft-skills, and emotional-intelligence are imperative for this course and the future.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

While it involves concepts and ideas completely new to me, I'm ready to embrace the culture and learning-environment -- and look to turn mistakes into learning, and take ownership in order to get the most out of the experience.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

Of course! I'm enjoying acclimatising, but it's great to see that EDA makes no half-measures when it comes to what kind of culture they want to build -- and build upon.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

Super excited, but also a little nervous.
