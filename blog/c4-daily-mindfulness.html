Daily Mindfulness

3 apr 18

What did you learn from your 'mindfulness conversation'? How did the other person find it?

From both perspectives, having the listener remain stoic / indifferent was really strange, and I felt less inclined to speak with emotion -- and it would be something to get used to.

I think the idea of a "mindulness" conversation is great / practical in many other terms, but it's not often seen as such. For example, generally just talking to family, or your partner could be seen as a "mindfulness" conversation, but the most consistent difference is for the listener to be more pro-active in "listening". This can affect the way you act / say things as the speaker; possibly more emotionally-attached to what you are saying.

Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?

Definitely having a buddy makes things easier, and a lot more sustainable; as having a partner for something unfamiliar generally makes things more comfortable
