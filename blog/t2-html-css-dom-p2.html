HTML, CSS, & DOM, pt.2, 15mar

  • What is grid based design?

    Separating a website's design into a series of grids allows us to see how to implement elements -- essentially serve as a guide for building a website.

  • Whats all the hype about responsive webpages?

    Responsive webpages, are webpages that respond to user's behaviour / environment -- such as changing the width of their browser. A responsive webpage would respond by dynamically changing all, or chosen elements of a webpage to accomodate this.

    This idea is commonly supported with the use of "media-queries", saying: if the screen size is less than x number of pixels (say, the max-width of a phone's display-resolution), the following CSS styles apply.

  • What is semantic structure?

    Markup "semantic structure", or "semantic structure", is the concept of presenting / laying-out your HTML in a way that makes it readable, easy to understand or reference. It's an important habit, and even moreso when working in teams.

  • Any other points you found interesting and want to reflect on.

    Building a webpage with a variety of technologies / frameworks was really interesting -- and it's easy to see why these frameworks are used; but with a combination of a framework such as Bootstrap and your own CSS, you can add your own personal touch to the design.
