Time and Habits, 11mar

  • Which timebox and habit techniques did you try?

    The Pomodoro Technique

    The idea of taking breaks intermittently is something I've found to work really well -- aiming to work with time, rather than against it, is super important in any field. There is a sense of accountability with setting a time-related goal -- whether it be 25-minutes, an hour, etc. -- that deters distractions, and gets the most out of your time. For instance, I used this technique with some success in 25-minute increments when tackling this sprint! Breaks did tend to be longer than 5-minutes, however...

  • What did you find interesting?

    Growth-mindset vs. Fixed-mindset

    This dichotomy of dynamic-versus-static learning-psychology really stood out. Especially for tackling phase-0, and eventually the physical-bootcamp, having a growth-mindset would be essential for getting the most out of any opportunity -- and not putting a "cap" on learning-potential.

    Starting small

    Breaking up goals into smaller increments, and having a MVP (minimum-viable-product) is essential for building towards an idea -- seeing a goal as lots of little processes, rather than one large process is super important (I think) for developers / entrepreneurs.

    With my own experience building small-scale apps for iOS, having to scale back an initial idea was essential in making measurable progress, and ultimately reaching a bigger goal.

    Building productive habits

    I took away a few key-points from Duhigg's and Sethi's conversation about habits:
    • In whatever it takes to get you somewhere, and to do it consistently -- do that, and your attitude will follow.
    • Look to ourselves for clues to who we are.
    • The cumulative net-effect of our behaviour(s), spills over into everything else in our lives -- that's when habit, creates change.

  • Which will you experiment further with?

    Pomodoro and the idea of small, incremental, reachable goals, are two concepts that I've used past, presently, and will aim to use and experiment with in the future.

    Further, I'm going to adopt a "growth-mindset", in order to continually improve and get better.
