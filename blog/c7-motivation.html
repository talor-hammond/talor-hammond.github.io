How does life look through the 'pleasure, passion and purpose' lens?

I think it's a good way of looking at the types of happiness in life: Pleasure is the most short-term, where most benefits are material. Happiness through passion seems to be the most sustainable, and happiness through a higher-purpose could be seen as an ultimate-goal, or even a by-product / collateral to one's passion.

From the 'discovering values and higher purpose' exercise: What are your core values? What do you stand for?

For core values: Honesty, integrity, unselfishness, generousity, family, respect.
I stand for my family, friends -- even when it may not be great for myself. Have a huge amount of respect for my grandparents, and try to do my best to give back to them.

In some way, I'm aimimng to try tie back something positive / productive to society for whatever I end up doing professionally.

How did you find the resilience ideas and meditation exercise?

The feeling of "re-experiencing" failure was really interesting: huge goosebumps (not the good kind), and uncomfortable sensations / feelings. Completely polar-opposite to the feelings derived from success. Ultimately, both of the feelings experienced fade when you realise these events have already happened -- but still found it to be interesting.
